Connor-Stevens Model

The Connor-Stevens model extends the Hodgkin-Huxley framework by adding a transient A-type potassium current, which helps model more complex neuronal firing patterns such as spike frequency adaptation and delayed firing.

Like Hodgkin-Huxley, this model describes membrane potential using nonlinear differential equations based on ion channel dynamics. Its key addition is the \( I_A \) current, which activates and inactivates rapidly.

The main equation is:

\( C_m \frac{dV}{dt} = A I_e - \bar{g}_L (V - E_L) - \bar{g}_K n^4 (V - E_K) - \bar{g}_{Na} m^3 h (V - E_{Na}) - \bar{g}_A a^3 b (V - E_K) \)

Where:

  • \( C_m \): Membrane capacitance
  • \( \frac{dV}{dt} \): Change in membrane potential over time
  • \( A \): Membrane area
  • \( I_e \): Injected current
  • \( \bar{g}_L \): Leak conductance
  • \( E_L \): Leak reversal potential
  • \( \bar{g}_K \): Potassium conductance
  • \( \bar{g}_{Na} \): Sodium conductance
  • \( \bar{g}_A \): A-type potassium conductance
  • \( E_K \): Potassium reversal potential
  • \( E_{Na} \): Sodium reversal potential
  • \( n, m, h \): Gate variables for potassium and sodium channels
  • \( a, b \): Activation and inactivation variables for A-type potassium channels

Components of the Model

The Connor-Stevens model includes the core components of Hodgkin-Huxley, with the addition of the transient A-type potassium current:

1. Current Injection (\( I_e \))
External current input used to simulate stimuli, just as in the HH model.

2. Leak Conductance (\( \bar{g}_L \))
Passive ion flow across the membrane through leak channels, contributing to the resting membrane potential.

3. Delayed Rectifier Potassium Current (\( \bar{g}_K \))
Helps return the membrane to rest after depolarization. This current increases as \( n \) activates during a spike.

4. Transient Sodium Current (\( \bar{g}_{Na} \))
Initiates the rapid upstroke of the action potential through voltage-gated sodium channels. Modeled using gate variables \( m \) and \( h \).

5. A-type Potassium Current (\( \bar{g}_A \))
Unique to the Connor-Stevens model. The \( I_A \) current activates quickly and inactivates shortly after, contributing to delayed firing and spike frequency modulation. It is defined as:

\[ I_A = \bar{g}_A \cdot a^3 b \cdot (V - E_K) \]

This A-type current allows neurons to have richer dynamics than those captured by the Hodgkin-Huxley model alone.
